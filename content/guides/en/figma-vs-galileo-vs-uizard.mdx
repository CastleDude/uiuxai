# Figma AI vs Galileo vs Uizard: Complete Comparison

## Who This Is For

Product managers, UI/UX designers, and founders who need AI-assisted rapid prototyping.

## What Problem This Solves

Too many AI prototyping tools, not enough clarity. Figma AI works within your existing workflow, Galileo AI excels at generating high-fidelity UIs from scratch, and Uizard turns sketches into prototypes. This guide helps you decide.

## Selection Criteria

Evaluate AI prototyping tools across 5 dimensions:

1. **Output Quality** — How close is the output to production-ready design?
2. **Learning Curve** — How fast can your team get started?
3. **Price** — Does it fit your budget?
4. **Collaboration** — Does it support team workflows?
5. **Ecosystem** — Does it integrate with your existing tools?

## Scenario Recommendations

### Quick Idea Validation → Galileo AI
One prompt, one high-fidelity mockup. Best for early-stage product exploration.

### Enhance Existing Figma Workflows → Figma AI
Zero switching cost if your team is already on Figma.

### Start from Hand Sketches → Uizard
Excels at turning whiteboard sketches into digital prototypes.

## FAQ

**Q: Which is best for beginners?**
A: Both Uizard and Galileo are extremely beginner-friendly. Uizard suits sketch-first designers, Galileo suits text-driven generation.

**Q: Can I use multiple tools together?**
A: Absolutely. Many teams use Galileo for rapid concept generation, then import into Figma and refine with Figma AI.
